During 14 hours of continuous rainfall near Mount Baker, the tent remained completely dry inside. The 1,500mm polyurethane coating and fully taped seams proved their worth. More importantly, the floor's bathtub design with 6-inch sidewalls prevented any splash-back during heavy downpours – a common failure point in lighter shelters.

The tent's wind resistance impressed me most during a bikepacking trip along the Columbia River Gorge. Known for its brutal wind conditions, the Gorge subjected the Copper Spur to sustained 25mph crosswinds with gusts reaching 40mph.
