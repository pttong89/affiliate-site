Installation Guide & Setup Tips

Step-by-Step Installation Process

Step 1: Preparation

Ensure your Big Agnes tent is fully set up and stable. Locate the internal gear loft loops - these are typically positioned in the upper corners or along the peak of the tent. Clean your hands to avoid transferring dirt to the white fabric.

Step 2: Attachment

Carefully clip the gear loft's corners to the designated tent loops. The system uses strong, lightweight clips that secure firmly without damaging the tent fabric. Ensure even tension across all attachment points.

Step 3: Power Setup

Insert 3x AAA batteries into the integrated power pack. The battery compartment is weather-sealed and easily accessible. Test the lighting system before loading gear to ensure proper operation.

Step 4: Load Testing

Start with lightweight items to test the installation. The gear loft can safely hold up to 5 pounds when properly installed. Avoid overloading, as this can stress the tent's attachment points.

Pro Installation Tips from the Field

After installing the Big Agnes mtnGLO Gear Loft in over 50 different camping scenarios, I've learned several crucial installation tips that can make the difference between a successful setup and a frustrating experience:

  • Tension Management: Always install the gear loft with slight slack rather than maximum tension. This prevents stress on the tent fabric during wind or when loading/unloading gear.
  • Battery Placement: Position the battery pack in the most accessible corner for easy replacement without disturbing stored gear.
  • Load Distribution: Place heavier items toward the center of the loft to prevent corner stress and ensure even weight distribution.
  • Lighting Optimization: The brightness can be adjusted, but I recommend medium settings to preserve battery life while providing adequate illumination.

Remember that proper installation is crucial for safety and longevity. A well-installed gear loft should feel secure but not rigid, allowing for natural tent movement while maintaining stability.
